Silly and Bad Ideas Session

  • An exercise in creative and critical thinking
  • How to get good ideas by having bad ideas
  • This should be fun!
  • Prof. Alan Dix, Director of Computational Foundry, Swansea University.

What do we meed by a bad idea?

  • Not something where the silliness is a matter of opinion
  • Not something real that was created with serious intentions, but that turned out to be silly
  • Just something original, but crazy, or clearly a bad idea
  • E.g. inflatable dart board, solar powered flashlight, chocolate teapot.

Turning bad ideas into good ideas - Review your Bad Ideas

  • Why bad ideas?
    • Less preconceptions: Harder to move along familiar paths to solve problems with good ideas
    • Less personal commitment: easier to hear critique of an idea that is deliberately bad
  • For any idea, you can ask:
    • What is good about it? Why?
    • What is bad about it? Why?
  • As it is (before turning a bad idea to good)
    • What is it optimal for? Can we find a context where the idea is good/better not bad/worse
    • Car salesman approach - how would you sell the idea?

Refining the Ideas

  • Make it a good idea:
    • What is good about it? - Keep these things
    • What is bad? - Change it
    • Change context - Sometimes changing the context rather than the idea will make it good
    • Learn from aspects - What can you learn about the problem space as a result of thinking about the bad idea(s).

Reviewing your Bad Ideas

Conceptual DescriptionThe GoodThe BadOptimal Context
A car powered by the heat of the road- Environmentally friendly - Don’t have to pay for fuel - Low maintenance (EV?)- Not very practical - Not ideal for cold places- Rural areas where there aren’t many gas stations
Superglue packaged in bottles- More environmentally friendly than plastic packaging - Easier to get large volumes of superglue for those that use it a lot- Difficult to apply to small work pieces- For people who have businesses etc that use a lot of superglue e.g. woodworking, electronics final assembly.
A tap that gives you salt water- Good for making pasta - Minimal processing required, especially since most cities are on the coastline - Removes the requirement for desalination which is energy intensive and expensive- Frequent pipeline maintenance required to remove salt build up on pipes- To use alongside a freshwater pipeline
Chocolate mug with no handle- Good for making cold drinks- Hot and messy if you intend to make a hot beverage using the cup- For making cold drinks or making a hot drink more interesting
Pool heated by a nuclear reactor- Basically never have to refuel it - Self contained pod for heating the pool- Would be hard to design a mechanism to control the temperature - Potential nuclear waste hazard- Rural areas where applications of water heating are required. - People that want to design houses that are off the grid
Keyboard that doesn’t have keycaps- Good for touch typing practice- Uncomfortable to type on- People that want to practice touch typing
Shoes made of metal- Very robust shoe design - Would offer good protection- Wouldn’t offer much grip - Potentially heavy and cumbersome to walk in - In hazardous areas where you don’t have to walk around much e.g. workshop setting
Glasses for blind people- Blind people are still sometimes blind, can we make something that improves their vision?- Glasses may not be especially helpful for blind people who absolutely can’t see- Unsure how to adapt this lol :)
Phone with mouse and keyboard- What if we add a portable mouse and keyboard to increase input speed and accuracy- Not terribly portable, annoying to carry extra things- Especially good for people who are travelling a lot
Car that doesn’t have any windows- Potentially more robust in a crash - Not very nice to be inside- For hazardous environments e.g. mineshafts. - Could be used for VIP protection
Messaging platform, but you don’t know who you’re messaging- Great place to meet new people - Semi-anonymised, so potential security issues. - Conversations may be a bit random- Especially good for international students / very introverted people to meet new friends.
Camera that doesn’t save or print photos- Could be used for online meetings as webcam - Could be used as a doorbell, streaming the feed.- Can’t share the videos or photos for later- Context of Use for a lightweight / simple camera that streams onto a network?

Refining the Ideas

Conceptual Description
Chocolate mug with no handleCreate a double-walled glass mug, with chocolate inserts. This way, you don’t have to touch a hot cup, but can still enjoy the chocolatey goodness.
Glasses for Blind PeopleWe could use the form factor of a set of glasses to hold a series of cameras that give wearer a 360 degree awareness of their surroundings. Use haptic feedback and a band around their head to signify different types of obstacles.
Phone with mouse and keyboardInstall a small projector into a phone - use a flat surface as an input device to increase both input speed and accuracy.
Car that doesn’t have any windowsRemove the windows in the car, but install displays in its place. Assuming the car can drive itself around, the user could toggle between a live view (using external cameras) or other media / interact with a computing device using the display.
